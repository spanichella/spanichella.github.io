Research Highlights & News

Selected highlights from my research activities, including scientific achievements, competitive research funding, international recognition, leadership roles, publications, and contributions to the advancement of Artificial Intelligence, intelligent software systems, and trustworthy cyber-physical systems.

  • Paper accepted at ESEM 2026. The paper "Characterising Meeting Practices in Software Engineering Teams: A Mixed-Methods Study of Perceptions, Objective Patterns, and Impacts" has been accepted at the International Conference on Empirical Software Engineering and Measurement (ESEM 2026), Technical Track.
  • Paper accepted in Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence (EAAI Journal 2026). The paper "Physics-Informed Machine Learning for Precision Unmanned Aerial Vehicle Control: Adaptive Transformers with Safety Guarantees" has been accepted for publication in the Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence journal.
  • Paper accepted in Information and Software Technology (IST Journal 2026). The paper "The Role of Road Features and Vehicle Dynamics in Cost-Effective Autonomous Vehicles Safety Testing: Insights from Instance Space Analysis" has been accepted for publication in Information and Software Technology.
  • Innosuisse Innovation Cheque project awarded. The project "mina App Gesundheitsreport / mina App Health Report", led by startup founder Michèle André, was awarded Innosuisse Innovation Cheque funding. The project explores AI-driven cycle data analytics to support women's health and well-being.
  • Appointed Head of the IDeaLS Lab at the Italian Institute of Artificial Intelligence for Industry (AI4I). Starting from February 2026, I will lead the Intelligent Development and Large-Scale Systems (IDeaLS) Lab at AI4I. The laboratory focuses on the foundations and applications of intelligent, adaptive, and trustworthy software systems for AI-enabled and cyber-physical systems.
  • Paper accepted at ICPC 2026. The paper "Understanding CI/CD Workflow Runs Through Interactive and Animated Visualizations" has been accepted at the International Conference on Program Comprehension (ICPC 2026).

Several research works co-authored by Sebastiano Panichella were included in the compilation "Most Cited Papers in Software Engineering 2013–2023" by Martin Monperrus (KTH Royal Institute of Technology).
