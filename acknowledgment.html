Research Journey & Acknowledgments

This page is a small acknowledgment of the people, mentors, colleagues, students, friends, and environments that shaped my personal and professional path.

THANK YOU!

Life is short, and we are extremely fortunate to have the opportunity to experience it. Throughout my journey, I have always tried to remember that "Love and work are the cornerstones of our humanness" (Sigmund Freud).

About my professional journey

Honoring Giovanni Capobianco: A Mentor Who Shaped My Path


Giovanni Capobianco (Professor at the University of Molise until his untimely passing in 2025) was a source of inspiration for many students and researchers.

His guidance and example had a lasting impact on my career and on the person I aspire to be as a teacher and researcher. The news of his passing, together with the touching messages shared by his students, reminded me of his warmth, patience, and deep passion for education.



I was fortunate to have Giovanni as one of my supervisors, together with Rocco Oliveto, during my bachelor thesis.

His guidance influenced not only my research approach but also my vision of what research and education should represent: passion, integrity, curiosity, and an uncompromising pursuit of quality.

I remain deeply grateful for the values and lessons he shared with me. Honoring his memory means carrying these teachings forward and trying to transmit them to the students and researchers I will meet throughout my career.

Thank you, Giovanni, for your example, your values, and your belief in the importance of research and education. It was a privilege to know and learn from you.

My Ph.D. studies at the University of Sannio represent a period of my life that I associate with the words "push" and "run".

I would like to thank Gerardo Canfora and Massimiliano Di Penta, who were probably the best advisors I could have had considering my skills and personality.

One of my closest collaborators and friends from the University of Sannio is Andrea Di Sorbo. We started working together during his Ph.D. studies and continued our collaboration over the years.

Andrea is creative, brilliant, and, more importantly, a person with strong ethical principles. His approach to research and collaboration represents a very positive example in an academic world that is sometimes challenging.

Thank you, Andrea.

More generally, I have many friends and colleagues from the University of Sannio and from abroad whom I met during my Ph.D. journey. There are too many people to mention individually, but I sincerely thank all of them for their support and for making me feel at home whenever I returned to UoS.

University of Bern: Freedom and Vision

University of Bern, my working environment as researcher and lecturer since autumn 2024, represents a new chapter of my professional and personal journey.

I associate this period with the words "freedom" and "vision": a phase where I aim to focus on realizing the person and researcher I would like to become.

UniBe represents a place where I can combine scientific curiosity, independence, collaboration, and long-term research ambitions.

I am particularly thankful to Timo Kehrer for creating an environment where this new chapter could develop.

This period represents a conceptual transition toward building a broader research vision, while embracing new challenges and opportunities for professional and personal growth.

UniBe has been the home of several successful projects, including:

- InnoGuard EU Project (Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions-funded Doctoral Networks project)

- SNF project SwarmOps "Human-sensing based MLOps for Collaborative Cyber-physical systems"

- Hasler Foundation project Safe-2-Fly "Advancing Unmanned Aerial Vehicles Reliability and Societal Trust Through Integrated Testing and Formal Verification"

- Innosuisse project BioAI4LCMS An interdisciplinary project combining biopharmaceutical R&D, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and software engineering.

- Hasler Foundation Aerialist "Bridging the Reality Gap in Testing Unmanned Aerial Vehicles"

These projects create opportunities for Ph.D. positions in Software Engineering and Robotics, particularly focusing on MLOps for AI-enabled Cyber-Physical Systems.

The objective is to develop cutting-edge approaches for AI-enabled Cyber-Physical Systems (AI-CPS), improving their adaptability, reliability, and sustainability in complex environments.

During these years, I also had the pleasure to work extensively with Pooja Rani, my former Ph.D. student at the University of Bern.

Since her second year of Ph.D. studies, where I acted as co-supervisor, we worked closely together. She impressed me with her determination, creativity, scientific maturity, and kindness.

I am confident that she will continue achieving important results in her career. She is currently Full Professor in Software Engineering at the University of Mannheim.

AI4I and the IDeaLS Lab: Leadership and Future Vision

AI4I – Italian Institute of Artificial Intelligence for Industry, my new working environment as Head of the IDeaLS Lab (starting February 2026), represents the next phase of my journey.

I associate this chapter with the words "vision", "autonomy", and "leadership".

After years dedicated to building my own research profile, this new phase represents an opportunity to build an environment where researchers can develop new ideas, collaborate across disciplines, and contribute to industrial innovation.

AI4I provides a unique combination of scientific excellence, industrial relevance, advanced computational resources, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

This represents a new professional and personal chapter in which I look forward to building and leading a research laboratory focused on reliable, sustainable, and intelligent software systems.

AI4I – the Italian Institute of Artificial Intelligence for Industry is the national AI center founded by the Italian Government to foster innovation, industrial transformation, and economic growth through application-oriented AI research.

With strong national support, access to advanced HPC infrastructure (including the Leonardo supercomputer at CINECA), and a large ecosystem of academic, industrial, and institutional partners, AI4I aims to become a key reference point for AI research and innovation in Italy and Europe.

IDeaLS Lab ("Intelligent Development and Large-Scale Systems Lab") will focus on:

- AI-assisted Software Engineering and MLOps for AI-enabled and Cyber-Physical Systems
- Methods, tools, and pipelines for development, testing, monitoring, and evolution of large-scale intelligent systems
- Dependability, explainability, security, and sustainability of AI-driven software ecosystems
- Interdisciplinary research connecting Software Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Life Engineering, Cloud Computing, Robotics, Smart Cities, and Intelligent Infrastructures

AI4I will be the home of projects such as:

- SNF project SwarmOps: Human-sensing based MLOps for Collaborative Cyber-physical systems

The IDeaLS Lab will welcome Ph.D. students and postdoctoral researchers in Software Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Robotics, and Cyber-Physical Systems.

The objective is to develop research that improves the adaptability, reliability, sustainability, and societal acceptance of intelligent systems operating in complex real-world environments.

From a personal and professional perspective, this step represents a natural continuation of my journey: an opportunity to contribute to AI4I's mission, develop a long-term research vision, and help shape the next generation of AI-enabled technologies.

Poems and Reflections

One of the things that has always fascinated me is how simple words can express deep human experiences. Among the poems I have read, I particularly appreciated a poem about parents attributed to Pablo Neruda. The following translated version of "When Parents Grow Old" contains a message that I find extremely meaningful:

"Let them grow old with the same love with which they raised you....

Let them repeatedly talk and tell stories with the same patience and interest with which they listened to yours when you were a child....

Let them win, as so many times they let you win....

Let them enjoy their friends, chatting with their grandchildren....

Let them enjoy living among the objects that have accompanied them for a long time, because they suffer feeling that you tear pieces of their lives away from them....

Let them be wrong, as so many times you have been wrong....

LET THEM LIVE

and try to make them happy during the last stretches of the path they have left to walk, the same way they gave you their hand when you were beginning yours."

Inspired by this poem, I wrote the following open letter to my mother:

Dear Mom,

Enjoy your mature years with the same love with which you loved us and allowed us to grow.

Tell us the stories of your life; we will listen to them with the same interest with which you listened to ours when we were children.

Enjoy your friendships, your conversations with relatives, and all the people close to you.

Keep close to you the things and values that have accompanied you for so long. They are important pieces of your life.

Feel free to travel, as many times you and Dad allowed us to discover new places and experiences.

Allow yourself to be happy, in the same way that you and Dad allowed us to be happy and always gave us a helping hand whenever we needed support.

I love you with all my heart.

Your son,

Sebastiano
